Here are just a few samples of my work. The colorful page is a sample from my high school yearbook, where I edited, took photos and created layouts.

The newspaper clippings are from my summer internship at The Osprey Observer when I was in high school. This internship was where I first got a taste of journalistic-style writing.

The first story I wrote was about a local woman who shared her story with me about the trials and great joys that came with adopting children from Russia, and how they have a reunion every two years. It was a heartwarming story and one of my first interviews I personally conducted for the internship.

This was one of the more fun stories I was able to cover for The Osprey Observer. I interviewed a local student who went to my high schopl about the charity founded to help Big Cat Rescue, where abused and injured cats are rehabilitated. Although I still had much to learn about writing hard news, I worked to get as much interesting and relevant information as I could.

The final article here that I wrote for The Osprey Observer was one of the more difficult. It involved heavy emotions for to the cause of fallen soldiers and their families. Many facts had to be collected

The final sample is a news release that was sent out to the media on behalf of an event for University Lutheran Church. This press release notified local media about the event and was actually published in The Gainesville Sun.

More of my recent stories have been published in The Independent Florida Alligator. For the first article, I localized a national story about a more gender-inclusive law being passed in California schools. The second was an event preview for the Ringling Bros. Circus coming to the UF campus for the first time.
