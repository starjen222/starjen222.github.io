UF Christian Campus House

This page is dedicated to my endeavors with UF Christian Campus House.

My journey of faith led me to this organization last year. I felt as though I was unfulfilled and longing for a greater aspect of family, of service, and of mission-oriented community.

Christian Campus House has reinvigorated that purpose within me and has provided me with some of the greatest opportunities for spiritual, relational, and professional growth.

My involvement at CCH has deepened tremendously since when I first joined. I came in as a passive observer, new to faith and with stereotypical views of what Christianity, church and religion were supposed to be.

Now, as a servant leader, director of service opportunities, and a future director of outreach, I see my involvement as a precursor to a career of missions, service, and creative communication.

Through my efforts of strategic communication across campus, dedicated mission work (whether abroad or right here in Gainesville), and passionate service, I have become part of what I like to call the new age of faith

Faith isn’t exclusion or hatred; faith is love and acceptance and reaching out to those in need; whether religion is a factor or not, I know that this is where I need to be

I made this video in addition to a story about two different outreach opportunities that I’m involved with at Christian Campus House: Downtown Outreach and English Corner.

Downtown Outreach is a weekly service opportunity at CCH in which students gather together to make peanut butter and jelly sandwiches to serve the homeless in Bo Diddley Plaza.

It’s an amazing opportunity. Students get real insight into the lives of others; the homeless are a people that society tends to shun or stigmatize. When students actually go downtown, however, they’re taught that spiritual nourishment is just as important as physical nourishment. In having real conversations with them, I see them as people with problems and hopes and fears, just as anyone else.

The second portion of the video describes English Corner. English Corner is one of my favorite extracurricular activities at my university. International students of any ethnicity or background are welcomed on Tuesdays and Thursdays to come to CCH for free-flowing and open dialogue about anything and everything. It is an amazing way to establish cross-cultural connections as well as learn more about different cultural perspectives, while at the same time how similar people are to one another despite these differences.
